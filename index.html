Competitiveness

Women in Patriarchal societies tend to become less competitive around puberty, while no change is seen in matriarchal societies. This same phenomena is seen in all girl’s schools versus co-educational schools, which indicates that male-dominant societies lead women to believe that they cannot compete as well as men can. Later in life, this is seen when women veer away from majors that are presumably difficult, like Computer Science.

  How does our own history look?

Early History


  • The University of Illinois was founded as an all boy’s school in 1867
  • Women were admitted into the University in 1870
  • However, even after admission into U of I, not all majors were open to women
  • Since then, U of I has made efforts to support its female students, such as creating a Women’s League (1896), a Women’s League (1898), and Gender Women Studies classes (1970s). These efforts must continue!

As a coeducational school, the University of Illinois must recognize that America has traditionally been extremely patriarchal. It must, therefore, work to empower its female population so that they are put on equal footing with the male counterparts—specifically in majors like Computer Science where they may be underrepresented or even oppressed.
